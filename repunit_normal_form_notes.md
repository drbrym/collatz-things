# Repunit Tail Normal Form

**Building on:** `repunit_tail_attack.md`, `repunit_bad_automaton_notes.md`
**Status:** Exact algebraic reformulation, verified experimentally. This is not yet a descent proof.

---

## 1. Normal form

Let

$$
x_0=a_n=\frac{3^n-1}{2},
\qquad
x_{i+1}=f(x_i),
\qquad
E_i=\sum_{j<i}v_2(3x_j+1).
$$

Then the repunit tail has the form

$$
x_i=\frac{3^{n+i}+A_i}{2^{E_i+1}},
$$

where

$$
A_0=-1,
\qquad
A_{i+1}=3A_i+2^{E_i+1}.
$$

The next valuation is therefore

$$
e_i
=v_2(3x_i+1)
=v_2\!\left(3^{n+i+1}+A_{i+1}\right)-(E_i+1).
$$

This is exact. It separates the moving exponential `3^n` from a correction term `A_i` controlled only by the previous valuation ledger.

---

## 2. Why this helps

The condition for a large payout at step `i` is now an explicit congruence:

$$
3^{n+i+1}\equiv -A_{i+1}\pmod {2^{E_i+1+t}}.
$$

So the repunit tail can be viewed as a moving target problem in the cyclic subgroup generated by `3` modulo powers of two.

This is stronger than the naive bad-automaton view. The naive view counts independent bad patterns. The normal form remembers how the correction term `A_i` evolves, and it exposes the exact congruence that must be hit to keep the valuation ledger low or to create a compensating payout.

---

## 3. Immediate observations

`A_i` is always odd:

$$
A_{i+1}=3A_i+2^{E_i+1},
$$

with `A_i` odd and `2^{E_i+1}` even.

Thus `-A_{i+1}` is always an odd 2-adic unit. Large payouts occur only when that unit lies close to the current power `3^{n+i+1}` in the 2-adic metric.

The missing proof ingredient can now be phrased as:

> the recurrence for `A_i` cannot keep arranging small 2-adic coincidences with `3^{n+i+1}` for `3n` consecutive steps without eventually forcing a large enough payout.

That is a much more precise target than “the post-repunit orbit is generic”.
