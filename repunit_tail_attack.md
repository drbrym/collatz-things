# Repunit Tail Attack

**Building on:** `mersenne_repunit_reduction.md`, `stopping_time_density.md`, `recharge_nogo.md`
**Status:** Exact reduction plus empirical proof target. This is not a proof of the Mersenne epoch.

---

## 1. The remaining target

For odd `n`, the Mersenne spine reaches

$$
a_n=\frac{3^n-1}{2}
$$

after exactly `n` odd-steps. Thus the remaining Mersenne epoch problem is:

$$
\sigma_n=\min\{K\ge1:f^K(a_n)<2^n-1\}.
$$

If one can prove a linear bound

$$
\sigma_n \le Cn
$$

for all odd `n`, then the Mersenne spine has linear epoch:

$$
\operatorname{epoch}(2^n-1)\le (1+C)n.
$$

This would not prove Collatz, but it would close the currently isolated worst-case spine.

---

## 2. Exact first payout

The first valuation after landing at the repunit is explicit. Since

$$
3a_n+1=a_{n+1},
$$

and

$$
v_2(a_m)=v_2(3^m-1)-1,
$$

LTE gives, for odd `n`,

$$
v_2(3a_n+1)=v_2(a_{n+1})=1+v_2(n+1).
$$

So the repunit tail does not begin with a random valuation. It begins with a forced payout controlled by `n+1`. After that first step, the exact repunit ladder is broken, and the orbit becomes generic-looking.

---

## 3. The target ledger

Let

$$
x_0=a_n,\qquad x_{i+1}=f(x_i),\qquad e_i=v_2(3x_i+1),\qquad E_K=\sum_{i<K}e_i.
$$

The affine accumulation formula gives

$$
x_K=\frac{3^K a_n+c_K}{2^{E_K}},
$$

with `c_K>0`. Therefore descent below the Mersenne start is exactly

$$
\frac{3^K a_n+c_K}{2^{E_K}}<2^n-1.
$$

Ignoring the positive affine tail gives the natural raw surplus

$$
S_K
=E_K-K\log_2 3-\log_2\!\left(\frac{a_n}{2^n-1}\right).
$$

For moderate and large `n`, this raw surplus is numerically almost identical to the exact margin

$$
\log_2\!\left(\frac{2^n-1}{x_K}\right),
$$

at first descent. Thus the proof target is sharply:

> show that the cumulative valuation sequence of the repunit tail cannot remain below the line
> `K log2(3) + log2(a_n/(2^n-1))` for too long.

Since

$$
\log_2\!\left(\frac{a_n}{2^n-1}\right)
= n\log_2(3/2)-1+o(1),
$$

and a generic orbit has mean valuation `2`, the mean-field prediction is

$$
K\approx
\frac{\log_2(3/2)}{2-\log_2 3}\,n
=1.40942\ldots n.
$$

That matches the observed center of the data.

## 4. Empirical window

`explore_repunit_tail.py` checks the exact tail, not a random model. It currently finds:

* for odd `n=7..2001`, every repunit tail satisfies `sigma_n <= 3n`;
* the worst ratio in that range is `sigma_23/23 = 63/23 = 2.739...`;
* the smallest exact descent margin found in that range is about `0.00122` bits, at `n=1345`.

The margin can be very small. So a proof of `sigma_n <= 3n` cannot rely on generous slack; it needs a structural reason that the valuation walk eventually crosses the target line.

Fixed windows below `3n` do not look safe:

* `1.5n` has large negative surplus in tested ranges;
* `2.0n` still has large negative surplus;
* `2.5n` still fails in small tested examples;
* `3.0n` is the first clean empirical window so far, excluding the tiny exceptional case `n=5`.

## 5. What would count as progress

A plausible theorem target is:

> **Repunit Tail Lemma.** For every odd `n>=7`, the orbit of `a_n=(3^n-1)/2` satisfies
> `f^K(a_n)<2^n-1` for some `K<=3n`.

Equivalently, prove that the repunit tail cannot shadow the lower-tail valuation set through `3n` steps.

This is much narrower than Collatz. It attacks only the post-Mersenne spine. It is also stronger than the density theorem in the needed direction: density says almost all points leave the bad set; the lemma would say this particular explicit family leaves it.

The present obstruction is clear. The Terras/Everett density theorem controls the size of the bad residue classes, but `a_n` is a sparse sequence and could, in principle, lie in those classes infinitely often. The missing ingredient is a non-concentration statement for the sequence

$$
a_n=\frac{3^n-1}{2}
$$

against the nested low-valuation residue classes generated by the shortcut map.
